Things actively losing you bookings today

Each item below is independently verifiable in the live site or Google search results. Most are 5-minute changes inside Squarespace settings — nothing here needs a developer.

1Your site appears on Google as "Your Site Title"

The page title that Google shows in search results — for every single page on the site — is the literal Squarespace placeholder text "Your Site Title". I confirmed this in two places:

  • The HTML source: <title>Your Site Title</title>
  • Live Google results: the homepage shows up as "Your Site Title", and /academy as "Aesthetic Training Academy | Expert Courses | Royal Aesthetic — Your Site Title"
Why it matters: people searching "lip filler London" see a generic placeholder where your brand should be. They scroll past. This is the single highest-impact fix on the entire site.
Fix: Squarespace → Settings → SEO → Site Title. Set to e.g. Royal Aesthetic London | Lip Filler, Anti-Wrinkle & Profhilo Clinic — Edgware & Cavendish Square. The empty meta description, the placeholder og:title, twitter:title and the schema name all auto-update from this. Also: change the Open Graph image (it's still a "50% off" promo banner — that's what shows when someone shares your site on WhatsApp or Facebook).
Live Google search: site:royalaesthetic.co.uk. Every result reads "Your Site Title".

The HTML source confirms it

If you're curious to verify yourself, right-click the homepage → View Page Source. Line 14 is the smoking gun. Open Graph and Twitter tags share the same placeholder, so link previews on WhatsApp / Instagram / Facebook all read "Your Site Title" too.

View-source on the live homepage. The empty meta description on line 12 is the second SEO killer.

2URL typos are indexed by Google

Your sitemap currently has these live URLs:

  • /lip-filers-2 — should be lip-fillers (missing one L, and the -2 screams "duplicate page")
  • /profphilo-face — should be profhilo-face (extra P)

Plus several near-identical duplicates: /lip + /lip-filers-2 + /lips-filler-price; /membership + /membership-1; /terms-and-conditions + /terms-and-conditions-1; /brazilian-bum-lift + /brazilian-bum-lift-1; /profhilo-face + /profphilo-face.

Why it matters: Google can't tell which version is the "real" page, so it splits your ranking power across both. Worse, the typos make you look unprofessional in search results.
Fix: in Squarespace pick one canonical version per service, fix the slug spelling, and 301-redirect duplicates to the canonical (Settings → URL Mappings: /lip-filers-2 -> /lip-fillers 301).

3The wrong text is in your H1

Your homepage's H1 — the most important heading for SEO — is currently SPECIAL OFFERS. Your actual brand promise — "Natural results. Medical-grade aesthetics. Personalised care." — isn't in any heading tag at all.

The screenshot to the right shows a different problem too: two CTAs side by side that compete for attention — we'll come back to that as item #9.

Fix: restructure so the hero headline is an H1 with treatment + location keywords (e.g. "Natural-result lip filler & aesthetic treatments — Edgware & Oxford Street, London"). Demote "Special Offers" to H2.
Hero section above the fold. The visible text is good copy — it's just not in an H1.

5Edgware location is hidden on the Contact page

The homepage advertises two locations (Edgware HA8 + Cavendish Square W1G). The contact page only shows Cavendish Square. People searching for a clinic in Edgware bounce.

Fix: add both addresses to /contact with separate Google Maps embeds, and create separate LocalBusiness schema entries per branch. Bonus — register a Google Business Profile per location. This is the single biggest local-SEO unlock you have available.

6No medical credentials on a medical-aesthetic site

The site repeatedly says "medical-grade products" and "highly experienced clinician" but never names: practitioner name + qualifications (NMC pin / GMC number / Save Face / JCCP membership), insurance / regulator, training body badges, or CQC registration.

Why it matters: post Botched-Britain coverage, UK aesthetic clients heavily filter by "is this person qualified?". Clinics on your block that show Save Face / JCCP badges convert visibly better. A careful prospect googling you can't currently find any independent verification, so they pick a competitor who has it on their homepage.
Fix (quick): "Meet Roxana" section with photo, qualifications, years experience, regulator badges, register profile link. Fix (longer): if you're not on Save Face / JCCP yet, applying is worth it for the badge alone (~£200/yr — pays for itself in a single booking).

7You have a 5.0 ★ Treatwell rating — nowhere on your site

You have a 5.0 rating with 12 reviews on Treatwell and you're not surfacing it anywhere on royalaesthetic.co.uk. This is your single best trust asset and it's hidden.

Fix this week:
  • Add a "Reviews" strip on the homepage with the star rating + 3 quoted reviews + a "Read all on Treatwell" link
  • Use Squarespace's testimonial block (or the Elfsight widget you already have loaded) to pull live reviews
  • Add Review / AggregateRating schema markup so the stars show up in Google search results next to your listing

8Service pages have prices — but nothing to sell the treatment

The Lip Filler page lists six options in a table (£150–£300) but has no:

  • Treatment description (what is "Russian" vs "Cherry Lips"? Most prospects don't know)
  • Before/after photos
  • Recovery / aftercare info
  • Practitioner name
  • FAQs ("Will it hurt?", "How long does it last?", "Can I have it before a wedding?")
  • Reviews
  • "What to expect on the day"
Why it matters: prospects who land here from Google have high intent but low information. Without descriptions, the page feels like a menu in a language they don't speak — they leave to find a clinic that explains it. Pages that do have FAQ content rank higher because Google rewards depth.
Fix: for each of your 5 service pages add 4 sections — What it is, Before / after, What to expect, FAQ (5-7 questions pulled from real DMs you've answered). This same content doubles as the knowledge base for the chatbot in Phase 1, so it's never wasted work.
Lip Filler price page. Six SKUs, prices, no context.

9The hero is doing two jobs and confusing visitors

Two CTAs sit side by side: "Free Consultation" (WhatsApp) and "Book Treatment" (FacesConsent). For a first-time visitor it's not clear which one is for them. See the hero screenshot above — both buttons have the same visual weight.

Fix: label by intent, not action. Primary: "New here? Free 10-min WhatsApp consult". Secondary: "Returning client? Book your treatment". 95% of new leads come via Instagram DM — the WhatsApp path should be the visually dominant one for cold traffic.

10Package pages don't break out what's included

"Gold Full Face Package" and "Natural Look Package" are mentioned on the homepage but don't show: total saving vs buying treatments separately, per-item value breakdown, or photos of typical results.

Fix: add a comparison table showing list price vs package price + savings. People don't buy a £600 package without seeing the maths.

11Image SEO: 21 of 23 homepage images have empty alt text

Plus your logo <img> has alt text that's the placeholder "Your Site Title".

Fix: in Squarespace click each image and write descriptive alt text — e.g. "Lip filler before and after at Royal Aesthetic London Edgware clinic". Helps blind / screen-reader users (legal requirement), unlocks Google Image search traffic, reinforces your keyword set.

12Schema markup is broken

Your LocalBusiness schema has empty address and empty openingHours fields, despite the real values being shown on the page. Your WebSite schema name is "Your Site Title".

Fix: Squarespace → Settings → Business Information: fill in address + opening hours. Once you fix the site title (item 1), these auto-update.

14Homepage HTML is 500KB and ships dozens of full-resolution photos

Your TTFB (server response time) is fast (157ms — Squarespace's CDN is doing well), but page weight is heavy because Squarespace ships full-res photos and the homepage has many of them. On 4G this is a slow first paint.

Fix: in Squarespace each image block has a quality toggle — set non-hero images to "web". Reduce homepage image count: if you have 15+ photos above the testimonial section, cut to 6-8.

16Two-location SEO: split into geo pages

Right now there's one site for "Royal Aesthetic" with two addresses footnoted. To rank for "lip filler Edgware" and "lip filler Oxford Street", you want two location-specific landing pages: /edgware and /cavendish-square (or /oxford-street). Each targets local search differently and stops the locations cannibalising each other.

17Brand-name confusion with another clinic

There's a competing clinic at royal-aesthetics.co.uk (Tunbridge Wells, hyphenated, plural). They rank when people search "Royal Aesthetics". Lean hard into "Royal Aesthetic London" as a search term — buy the .com if available, use it consistently across IG bio, Treatwell, GMB, and outbound links to differentiate.

18The site doesn't capture leads who aren't ready to book

A first-time visitor researching lip filler in London might come back 3–4 times before booking. Right now the site has zero way to capture them between visits — no email signup, no "send me the price list", no "Free 10-min consult guide" lead magnet.

Fix idea (light-touch): single email-capture popup that triggers after 30 seconds: "Get our 1-page lip filler guide — what to expect, how to choose, prices." Even at 2% capture rate on your monthly traffic, that's 5–10 emails/month feeding into Phase 2's birthday + reactivation flows. Phase 2 of the proposal needs this lead source to work.
